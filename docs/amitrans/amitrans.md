# Am I transgender?
<p>
If you came here expecting an answer to the question above, I'm sorry to say, but I don't have it. Only <b>you</b> are able to answer that question. However, I still may be able to help you find that answer.
</p>

<p>
I'll start by explaining how I discovered that I am trans, and then I'll ask you some questions to consider. I am a trans woman, hence the following two paragraphs are written from that perspective, however, feel free to replace words if it helps you relate better.

&nbsp;I first realized that I may be trans when I was hanging out with some friends. I was just chilling out, and then suddenly the thought popped into my head and wouldn't leave. I began noticing how envious I was of women, their clothes, their hair styles, and etc. For weeks I felt very confused, afraid, and lost. I had no idea what to do. Eventually, I decided to start experimenting with my gender. I started by shoddily photoshopping long hair and makeup on myself, and that made me really happy. Not long after I bought myself some feminine clothes, a cute t-shirt and some leggings, and putting them on made me ecstatic. 

&nbsp;It is completely normal to have doubts. When I made my first appointment to get hormones, it made me feel very excited and hopeful. However, as the appointment got closer, I began having doubts if I am actually trans. However, I still went in and got prescribed hormones. Afterwards, I had family members tell me that I looked happier than they have ever seen before. I remember quite vividly how insanely happy I was to pick them up from the pharmacy and take them for the first time. Whenever I have doubts, I remember all the times I experienced gender euphoria and how much happier I am now compared to before I transitioned. Don't feel bad if you are doubting yourself, it is completely normal, especially if you don't have a support system.
</p>
