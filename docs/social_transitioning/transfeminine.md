# Transfeminine Social Transitioning
{: .no_toc }

Transfeminine is an umbrella term for transgender people who were assigned male at birth and are partially or fully feminine. They may be women, nonbinary, genderfluid, or any other gender that a feminine person who is AMAB may have. It is entirely up to you to determine if you are transfeminine or not. 

This page was written primarily for people who have recently determined that they are transfeminine and are looking for information about how to transition. There is no one way to transition and what matters in the end is that you are happy and content with yourself.

## Pronouns

Many transfeminine people use she/her pronouns. Some may also use they/them, neopronouns, a combination (e.g., she/her and they/them), or any pronouns. Some people may decide to not change their pronouns and stick with using he/him. It is up to you if you want to change your pronouns and what to change them to. Your decision is not permanent and if you end up not liking the pronouns you chose you can totally change them again. If you aren't sure and feel safe to do so, you can ask people to try using the pronouns you think you might like to see how you feel being called them. Even if you are sure, it can still be very euphoric to be called the pronouns you want to be called. There are many online communities for transgender and gender nonconforming people that would be happy to help you try out pronouns as well.

## Name

Some transfeminine people choose to change their name and some don't. If they do change their name, it is typically to a feminine or gender neutral name. Some may look online at lists of names, ask their parents to pick their name, pick a name from their favorite video game or movie, or choose a name that they heard long ago that has stuck with them. There are many ways to pick a name, or maybe you don't pick a new name at all, and whatever decision you make is completely valid. Your decision isn't permanent and if you decide you don't like the name you have anymore, you can choose a different one. If you do decide to change your name, it is common to end up with multiple names you can't decide between. To narrow down your list, try out each name to see how much you like it. There are many ways to try out names, such as using them in a video game, on a delivery address, or by asking friends and family to use them. Of course, only do what you feel safe and comfortable doing. There are also many online communities for transgender and gender nonconforming people that would be happy to help you try out names as well.

## Appearance

It is very common for transfeminine people to change at least some aspect of their appearance, be it clothes, accessories, makeup, hair, etc. Just because someone is transfeminine, it does not mean that they have to be a stereotypical girly-girl, although they can be that if they want to. They also do not have to give up any traditionally masculine things that they enjoy. What is important is that they are happy with themselves and feel affirmed in their gender. If you aren't sure if you want to change part of your appearance or what to change it to, that is fine and it is completely normal to experiment with clothes, makeup, hair, etc. Drugstores are typically a good place to go to get cheaper makeup if that is something you want to try out. Additionally, you can also get cheaper clothes to try out from thrift stores or you can just window shop if you like. If you are interested in skintight clothing, you may also be interested in tucking and gaffs. 

### Tucking, Gaffs, & Tape

If you are wanting to achieve a smooth appearance in the groin region, there are a few ways to do so without surgery. The most common way is tucking, which other methods also make use of. To do this, you will need to push your testicles into your inguinal canals. You have one on the left and right side of your pelvis and they are where your testicles originally descended from. Now that your testicles are out of the way, you can push your penis down towards your perineum. Do not pull and stretch it tight; just push it down. To help secure it in place so it doesn't come untucked, you'll want to use either a gaff, tight underwear (but not so tight it hurts), or tucking tape. Tucking should not be painful. If it is, you should untuck and wait until it no longer hurts to try again. If you decide to use tucking tape, be aware that it is a specific type of tape. Do <b>not</b> use packing tape, duct tape, etc. <b>Only</b> use tucking tape or tape intended to be used on sensitive skin areas. Tucking tape, however, has a few caveats that make it undesirable in many situations. Tucking tape is a disposable item, not the easiest to put on, and every time you need to pee you will have to take it off. In addition to that, it can be uncomfortable or even painful to take off for some given that the penis is a sensitive area. Due to those reasons I strongly recommend a gaff instead, especially if you are going to be tucking frequently. Gaffs are essentially just compression underwear to help flatten out your front. With gaffs you may be able to achieve a smooth appearance without tucking, but if you can't you can still tuck with the gaff on. If you can not afford a gaff, an alternative is to use tight underwear instead, but it shouldn't be overly tight, just tight enough to help secure your tuck.

## Voice

For transfeminine people, taking feminizing hormones will not feminize your voice. If you want to change your voice, regardless of whether you plan on taking feminizing hormones or not, you will either have to do voice training and/or get surgery. Surgery is not necessary although it can be helpful for some people. If you want to feminize your voice, you should try to do so by voice training first before deciding to get surgery. Some great Youtube channels to get you started are [TransVoiceLessons], [Seattle Voice Lab], and [Undead Voice Lab]. There are many others you can use as well and some even have personalized lessons. If you have a gender services provider, they should also be able to help you find resources to feminize your voice.

There are three primary aspects of your voice to change in order to feminize it. These are pitch, vocal weight, and resonance. Essentially, what you are trying to do is heighten pitch, raise your larynx, and shrink the space in your throat. Doing all of that right from the get-go is very difficult, so focus on just one aspect at a time. It is important to use guides to make sure you aren't doing something that is actually harming your voice.

### Pitch

Pitch is the one you probably already know. It is how deep or high your voice is. If you've tried feminizing your voice before without looking for guides, you've probably tried by just raising your pitch and changing nothing else. If you try doing this now, you may notice that you can't go very high or that it doesn't really sound feminine, which is because you're only just starting and there are two other aspects to your voice as well. Pitch also isn't really that important compared to the other two aspects either. When raising the pitch of your voice it is important to <b>not</b> use your falsetto. Your falsetto is the highest pitch you can go and you should not be using it as your normal speaking voice. When raising your pitch, you will notice a little pause or a jump before you reach your falsetto. When you first try raising your pitch, it might be difficult to get it to the pitch you want and this can be very discouraging, but just keep trying. My suggestion is to speak in the highest pitch you <b>comfortably</b> can 24/7, which might not be that much higher than your current speaking voice, but doing this should gradually allow you to raise your pitch up. This is what I did when I first started voice training, but if it doesn't work for you, there are other exercises you can do to help raise your pitch. A common one is to speak or hum in ascending and descending pitches.

### Vocal Weight

Vocal weight is how "heavy" or "light" your voice is. This might be hard to grasp at first without an aural demonstration, but basically, it is why feminine voices are described as "flute-like" or "smooth" and masculine voices are described as "gravelly" or "rough." If you still don't quite understand what vocal weight is, I recommend watching a video from one of the above Youtube channels on vocal weight, as it is easier to explain aurally than through text. When working on your vocal weight, some common mistakes are making your voice too breathy or constricting it too much. I made the mistake of having my voice too breathy for about a week, until I got feedback from people around me that it sounded strange, which caused me to check it with a video from one of the above Youtube channels. You'll know if your voice is too breathy or constricted if you fatigue easily from not much speaking. To feminize your vocal weight (reducing it), you will want to make your voice lighter. An exercise that helped me is to sing "aaaaaaa" and try to make it sound light and clear. Once you figure out how to do so, try singing in different pitches while keeping your vocal weight the same. You can go from low to high or high to low or both.

### Resonance

Resonace is very important for making your voice sound feminine. It is essentially where your voice is coming from. You can actually feel your resonance by putting your hand to your throat or upper chest and speaking. Wherever the vibration is the strongest is where your resonance is. Masculine voices typically resonate from the upper chest to lower throat and feminine voices typically resonate from the upper throat to the nose. To feminize your voice, you will want to brighten your resonance by raising your larynx. Absolutely do <b>not</b> do this by physically grabbing it and moving it up. While this will temporarily brighten your resonance, you risk acquiring dysphagia (difficulty swallowing). It is, however, perfectly safe to use the muscles in your neck to move your larynx up; you actually do this all the time while speaking. When you brighten your resonance, you will notice that your voice sounds noticeably more feminine even if you haven't worked much on the other two aspects yet. I found that I actually unintentionally brightened my resonance while working on the other two aspects, so I don't have much experience with the resonance exercises since I didn't really need to do them, but I'll still list one that I know of here. This exercise I first heard about from TransVoiceLessons and it is to breathe out, and then raise it so it's like a hiss instead. Then try keeping your larynx raised and speaking.

Voice training is something that will take a few months to get the hang of and potentially a few years to perfect. This makes it feel like a monumental task and it especially does the first few weeks when your voice still sounds mostly masculine, but I encourage you to keep at it. Even though it is hard you totally can do it! :3
