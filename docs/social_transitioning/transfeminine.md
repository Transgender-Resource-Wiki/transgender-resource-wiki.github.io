## Voice

For transfeminine people, taking feminizing hormones will not feminize your voice. If you want to change your voice, you will either have to do voice training and or get surgery. Surgery is not necessary although it can be helpful for some people. If you want to feminize your voice, you should try to do so by voice training first before deciding to get surgery. Some great Youtube channels to get you started are [TransVoiceLessons], [Seattle Voice Lab], and [Undead Voice Lab]. There are many others you can use as well and some even have personalized lessons.

There are three primary aspects of your voice to change in order to feminize it. These are pitch, vocal weight, and resonance. Essentially, what you are trying to do is heighten pitch, shrink the space in your throat, and raise your larynx. Doing all of that right from the get-go is very difficult, so focus on just one aspect at a time. It is important to use guides to make sure you aren't doing something that is actually harming your voice.

### Pitch

Pitch is the one you probably already know. It is how deep or high your voice is. If you've tried feminizing your voice before without looking for guides, you've probably tried by just raising your pitch and changing nothing else. If you try doing this now, you may notice that you can't go very high or that it doesn't really sound feminine, which is because you're only just starting and there are two other aspects to your voice as well. It is important when raising the pitch of your voice to <b>not</b> use your falsetto. Your falsetto is the highest pitch you can go and you should not be using it as your normal speaking voice. When raising your pitch, you will notice a little pause or a jump before you reach your falsetto. When you first try raising your pitch, it might be a difficult to get it to the pitch you want and this can be very discouraging, but just keep trying. My suggestion is to speak in the highest pitch you <b>comfortably</b> can 24/7 which will gradually allow you to raise your pitch even more. This is what I did when I first started voice training, but if it doesn't work for you, there are other exercises you can do to help raise your pitch.

### Vocal Weight

Vocal weight is how "heavy" or "light" your voice is. This might be hard to grasp at first without an aural demonstration, but basically, it is why feminine voices are described as "flute-like" or "smooth" and masculine voices are described as "gravelly" or "rough." If you still don't quite understand what vocal weight is, I recommend watching a video from one of the above Youtube channels on vocal weight, as it easier to explain aurally than through text. When working on your vocal weight, some common mistakes are making your voice too breathy or constricting it too much. I made the mistake of having my voice too breathy for about a week, until I got feedback from people around me that it sounded strange, which caused me to check it with a video from one of the above Youtube channels. You'll know if your voice is too breathy or constricted if you fatigue easily from not much speaking. To feminize your vocal weight (reducing it), you will want to make your voice lighter. Some exercises to help you with this is to imagine that you are speaking to a cute animal or baby. When people do "baby-speak" they usually reduce their vocal weight in addition to raising their pitch. Another is to sing "aaaaaaa" and try to make it sound light and clear. Once you figure out how to do so, try singing in different pitches while keeping your vocal weight the same. You can go from low to high or high to low or both.

### Resonance

Resonace is very important for making your voice sound feminine. It is essentially where your voice is coming from. You can actually feel your resonance by putting your hand to your throat or upper chest and speaking. Wherever the vibration is the strongest is where your resonance is. Masculine voices typically resonate from the upper chest to lower throat and feminine voices typically resonate from the upper throat to the nose. To feminize your voice, you will want to brighten your resonance by raising your larynx. Absolutely do <b>not</b> do this by physically grabbing it and moving it up. While this will temporary brighten your resonance, you risk acquiring dysphaga (difficulty swallowing). It is however, perfectly safe to use the muscles in your neck to move your larynx up; you actually do this all the time while speaking. When you brighten your resonance, you will notice that your voice sounds noticeably more feminine even if you haven't worked much on the other two aspects yet. I found that I actually unintentionally brightened my resonance while working on the other two aspects, so I don't have much experience with the resonance exercises since I didn't really need to do them, but I'll still list one that I know of here. This exercise I first heard about from TransVoiceLessons and it is to breathe out, and then raise it so it's like a hiss instead. Then try keeping your larynx raised and speaking.

Voice training is something that will take a few months to get the hang of and potentially a few years to perfect. This makes it feel like a monumental task and it especially does the first few weeks when your voice still sounds mostly masculine, but I encourage you to keep at it. Even though it is hard you totally can do it! :3
