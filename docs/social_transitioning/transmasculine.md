# Transmasculine Social Transitioning
{: .no_toc }

Transmasculine is an umbrella term for transgender people who were assigned female at birth and are partially or fully masculine. They may be men, nonbinary, genderfluid, or any other gender that a masculine person who is AFAB may have. It is entirely up to you to determine if you are transmasculine or not. 

This page was written primarily for people who have recently determined that they are transmasculine and are looking for information about how to transition. There is no one way to transition and what matters in the end is that you are happy and content with yourself.

## Pronouns

Many transmasculine people use he/him pronouns. Some may also use they/them, neopronouns, a combination (e.g., he/him and they/them), or any pronouns. Some people may decide to not change their pronouns and stick with using she/her. It is up to you if you want to change your pronouns and what to change them to. Your decision is not permanent and if you end up not liking the pronouns you chose you can totally change them again. If you aren't sure and feel safe to do so, you can ask people to try using the pronouns you think you might like to see how you feel being called them. Even if you are sure, it can still be very euphoric to be called the pronouns you want to be called. There are many online communities for transgender and gender nonconforming people that would be happy to help you try out pronouns as well.

## Name

Some transmasculine people choose to change their name and some don't. If they do change their name, it is typically to a masculine or gender neutral name. Some may look online at lists of names, ask their parents to pick their name, pick a name from their favorite video game or movie, or choose a name that they heard long ago that has stuck with them. There are many ways to pick a name, or maybe you don't pick a new name at all, and whatever decision you make is completely valid. Your decision isn't permanent and if you decide you don't like the name you have anymore, you can choose a different one. If you do decide to change your name, it is common to end up with multiple names you can't decide between. To narrow down your list, try out each name to see how much you like it. There are many ways to try out names, such as using them in a video game, on a delivery address, or by asking friends and family to use them. Of course, only do what you feel safe and comfortable doing. There are also many online communities for transgender and gender nonconforming people that would be happy to help you try out names as well.

## Appearance

It is very common for transmasculine people to change at least some aspect of their appearance, be it clothes, accessories, makeup, hair, etc. Just because someone is transmasculine, it does not mean that they have to be a stereotypical manly-man, although they can be that if they want to. They also do not have to give up any traditionally feminine things that they enjoy. What is important is that they are happy with themselves and feel affirmed in their gender. If you aren't sure if you want to change part of your appearance or what to change it to, that is fine and it is completely normal to experiment with clothes, accessories, hair, etc. If you like makeup, it is perfectly okay to still wear it, and likewise, it is perfectly okay to not wear it if you don't like it. If you want to expirement with clothes, a thrift store is a good place to get cheaper clothes to try out or you can just window shop if you like. You may also be interested binders to achieve a flatter chest and packers to achieve a bulge.

### Binders & Tape

If you are wanting to achieve a flat chest, there are a few ways to go about it without surgery. One of the methods is to use a binder, which is a compressive undergarment used to flatten the chest. If you decide to use a binder, it is important to get one that is the right size. In addition to that, it should <b>not</b> be worn for over 8 hours at a time. Additionally, they should <b>not</b> be slept in nor should you exercise in them. When you first get a binder, you may want to only wear it for a few hours at a time so your body gets used to it. If wearing your binder hurts or is causing difficulty breathing, take it off as soon as possible. If binding is causing breathing difficulty, you should try a size up. Another way to achieve a flatter chest is to use binding tape. Binding tape is applied directly to the skin and can be worn for a few days at a time. If you are interesting in binding tape, check out this <a href="https://www.gendergp.com/trans-tape-how-to-use-chest-binding-alternative/">guide</a> (<a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20240216182250/https://www.gendergp.com/trans-tape-how-to-use-chest-binding-alternative/"><span style="font-size:0.8em;">Mirror on Wayback Machine</span></a>) on it.

### Packers

If you are wanting to have a bulge, this is typically done by using a packer. A packer is a prosthetic used in underwear to give the appearance of having a penis. Packers can easily be DIYed by using a bundle of socks or condoms filled with hair gel. There are also silicone packers that can be bought as well. These typically look like flaccid penises. There are even some models that are hollow with openings at each end, which makes it possible to urinate with them on. In addition to that, there are also briefs that are designed to be used with a foam insert if that's more your style. 

## Voice

For transmasculine people, taking masculinizing hormones will masculinize your voice. Transmasculine people who are planning on taking masculinizing hormones typically do not need to do voice training. If you are unable to take masculinizing hormones or do not want to, it is still possible to masculinize your voice using voice training if it is something you want. Some transmasculine people will do voice training even if they have taken masculinizing hormones to achieve an even deeper or heavier voice. Additionally, some transmasculine people may want to take masculinizing hormones, but not have their voice masculinize. If that is something you want, you can do voice training to achieve an androgynous voice or to regain a [feminine voice]. There are lots of voice training tutorials freely available on Youtube, and many of them also have personalized lessons available as well. If you have a gender services provider, they should also be able to help you find resources to feminize your voice.
